4.a Chris buys coffee for $1 per cup and tea for 50¢ per cup; every day she drinks 2 cups of coffee and 1 cup of tea. David buys coffee for 50¢ per cup and tea for $1 per cup; every day he drinks 1 cup of coffee and 2 cups of tea. Can you determine whether Chris and David have identical tastes?

Let coffee be “c”, tea be “t”.

As Christ buys (c, t) = (2, 1), his budget line is: c + 0.5t = 2.5

As David buys (c, t) = (1, 2), his budget line is: 0.5c + t = 2.5

Since (2, 1) and (1, 2) both are within Christ’s budget line, and Christ chooses (2, 1), we can know he perfers (2, 1) than (1 ,2).

Similarly, (2, 1) and (1, 2) both are within David’s budget line, and David chooses (1, 2), we can know he perfers (1, 2) than (2 ,1).

Hence, their indifferent curves must cross, i.e. Chris and David do not have identical tastes.

4.b Evelyn buys coffee for $1 per cup and tea for 50¢ per cup; every day she drinks 1 cup of coffee and 2 cups of tea. Frederick buys coffee for 50¢ per cup and tea for $1 per cup; every day he drinks 1 cup of coffee and 1 cup of tea. Can you determine whether Evelyn and Frederick have identical tastes?

As Evelyn buys (c, t) = (1, 2), her budget line is: c + 0.5t = 2

As Frederick buys (c, t) = (1, 1), his budget line is: 0.5c + t = 1.5

Since (1, 2) and (1, 1) both are within Evelyn’s budget line, and Evelyn chooses (1, 2), we can know she perfers (1, 2) than (1 ,1).

Since (1, 1) is within Frederick’s budget line but (1, 2) is not, we cannot sure whether Frederick perfers (1, 2) or (1, 1).

Hence, Evelyn and Frederick may or may not have identical tastes.

 

5.a Huey consumes only two goods, X and Y. His indifference curves have the usual shape (strictly convex). He prefers basket (1, 3) to basket (2, 2).

5.a.i Is it possible to tell whether Huey prefers (1, 3) to (3, 1)?

Yes, we can know Huey prefers (1, 3) to (3, 1).

5.a.ii Is it possible to tell whether Huey prefers (3, 1) to (2, 2)?

Yes, we can know Huey prefers (2, 2) to (3, 1).

5.b Dewey consumes only two goods, X and Y. His indifference curves have the usual shape (strictly convex). He prefers basket (1, 3) to basket (3, 1).

5.b.i Is it possible to tell whether Dewey prefers (1, 3) to (2, 2)?

No, we cannot know Dewey prefers (1, 3) to (3, 1), since there are two possibilities.

5.b.ii Is it possible to tell whether Dewey prefers (3, 1) to (2, 2)?

Yes, we can know Dewey prefers (2, 2) to (3, 1).
